Dear Editors,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, **"Lifestyle modification for type 2 diabetes
remission in low- and middle-income countries, with drug-free hypertension normalisation
as a secondary objective: a systematic review and meta-analysis protocol,"** for
consideration as a Protocol in *Systematic Reviews*.

Type 2 diabetes and hypertension are the leading drivers of non-communicable disease
mortality across low- and middle-income countries (LMICs), where the reliable, lifelong
pharmacotherapy that current management assumes often cannot be sustained. Over the past
decade, randomised trials in high-income settings have shown that structured lifestyle
modification can drive type 2 diabetes into sustained, **drug-free remission**. Yet this
evidence has been generated almost entirely in high-income populations, synthesised
without disaggregation by country income level, and never assembled into a systematic
evidence base for LMIC guidance. To our knowledge, no systematic review has used the 2021
international consensus definition of remission as its primary outcome in LMIC settings,
compared the baseline characteristics of LMIC trial populations with their high-income
counterparts, or examined sustained drug-free blood-pressure normalisation in these
settings.

Our protocol addresses these gaps directly. It restricts eligibility to randomised
controlled trials, applies the 2021 consensus definition as the primary outcome filter,
adds an exploratory hypertension-normalisation strand, and includes a structured
comparison of LMIC versus high-income participant profiles to interrogate the
generalisability of existing remission evidence. The work is grounded in our group's
published research programme on NCD financing and primary-care capacity in western Kenya,
which documented frequent essential-medicine stockouts and the near-absence of HbA1c
testing — the conditions that make drug-free remission a question of real consequence
for these health systems.

We confirm that:

- The protocol is prospectively registered with PROSPERO (CRD420261409919).
- It is reported in full accordance with the PRISMA-P 2015 guidelines (checklist provided
  as an additional file).
- The complete MEDLINE search strategy is provided as an additional file, and all
  analysis code will be deposited openly on the Open Science Framework prior to
  publication of the completed review.
- This manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and all named authors
  have approved the submission. The authors declare no competing interests.

We believe this protocol is well suited to the readership of *Systematic Reviews* and will
provide a methodologically rigorous foundation for an evidence base of direct clinical and
policy relevance to diabetes and hypertension care in resource-constrained settings.
